## Project Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition at Adecco India using Microsoft Excel.

The objective is to identify factors influencing employee turnover and recommend strategies to improve employee retention.

---

## Business Problem

Adecco India is experiencing high employee attrition, particularly among junior-level employees and within the Sales department.

The analysis aims to identify the key drivers of employee turnover and support data-driven HR decisions.

---

## Key Findings

### Overall Attrition Rate

- 16.1% of employees left the organization.

### Department Analysis

- Sales Department recorded the highest attrition rate (20.6%).

### Overtime Analysis

- Employees working overtime had a 30.5% attrition rate.
- Employees not working overtime had only a 10.4% attrition rate.

### Income Analysis

- Low-income employees had a 21.8% attrition rate.
- Very high-income employees had only a 3.8% attrition rate.

### Job Involvement Analysis

- Employees with low job involvement showed a 33.7% attrition rate.
- Employees with high job involvement showed only a 9.0% attrition rate.

---

## High-Risk Employee Profile

A high-risk employee is typically:

- Early-career
- Working in Sales
- Low income
- Working overtime
- Low job involvement
